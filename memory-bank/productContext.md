# Product Context — Underground Arena

## Why this exists
The mobile market is full of card battlers and idle fighters, but very few capture the **gritty, nostalgic Facebook-era combat game feel** (think old MMA/mafia/fighter Flash games). Underground Arena targets that emotional pocket: dark theme, raw fighters, satisfying progression, no social pressure.

## Problems it solves
- **"I just want a quick fight on the bus."** Sessions are bite-sized (one fight = 30–90s).
- **"Online games annoy me with logins/lag/microtransactions."** Fully offline, no account, no pay-to-progress.
- **"Card battlers feel slow."** Combat is automated and fast — the strategy lives in the deck builder, not in turn-by-turn micromanagement.
- **"I want constant rewards."** Pack drops, level ups, league unlocks, daily challenges, achievements — something is always unlocking.

## How it should work (player flow)
1. Player opens app → home screen with energy, currency, daily reward, next fight.
2. Picks a fight (career / tournament / survival / daily / event).
3. Watches an auto-resolved fight with clear combat log + animations.
4. Wins coins/XP, possibly a card or pack, possibly a card "discovery" hint.
5. Returns to home → builds deck, trains stats, opens packs, climbs leagues.
6. Energy gates pacing; daily rewards bring them back tomorrow.

## User experience goals
- **Feel powerful.** Crits, combos, ultimates, screen shake, color rarity flashes.
- **Feel rewarded.** Numbers go up frequently, especially in first session.
- **Feel ownership.** Their fighter, their deck, their record — built over time, persisted locally.
- **Feel respect.** No dark patterns, no forced ads in MVP, no nags.

## Tone & aesthetic
- Underground / illegal-fight-club vibe.
- Dark UI, neon/blood accents, gritty typography.
- Names like "Bruno 'The Hammer' Silva", "Mad Dog Reyes" — generated from data-driven name pools.
